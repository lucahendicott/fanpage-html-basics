Bio

Shania Twain is a "Pop Country" singer songwriter. Originally from Ontario, Canada. Twain began from a young ages in the early 90's. Her hit album "The Woman in Me" (1995) sent her career into a booming success. Albums to come after would only land her greater and greater success in the following years. In 2004, after releasing her greatest hits album, She took a break from the scene. After being diagnosed with Lyme disease and Dysphonia, her vocal abilities were extremely limited. Though, after much rehabilitation, Twain has recently been back in the scene! She has since released a new music and has been performing live around the country. Shania Twain has received five Grammy Awards, 27 BMI Songwriter Awards, stars on Canada's Walk of Fame and the Hollywood Walk of Fame, and an induction into the Canadian Music Hall of Fame.
